Foreign Relations

Nations can be involved in a number of different relationships.

War

When nations are at war, units are allowed to attack and auto-attack that player (auto-attack is controlled by field orders). There are two ways to start a war with another player. Both are accessible from the Foreign Relations window.
- Players can explicitly "Declare War".
- Players can setup "sneak attack" orders against another player. When the sneak attack happens, both nations switch to "at war".

Neutral

When a nation has a neutral stance towards another nation, its units will not attack that player. However, if that other nation attacks, your own foreign policy will automatically switch to "war".

Alliance

When nations are allied, their units will not attack each other. Additionally, allies can land/dock units in each other's cities, and units can be repaired and upgraded there.

Alliances can be broken at any time. This means former allies can become enemies.

Alliances are created through the message / trade agreement system.

Team

A Team is similar to an Alliance, except that a team can contain an unlimited number of players, is permanent, and allows players (in the team) to win the game together.

Teams are created through the foreign relations / team window.

Teams can be setup inside a scenario. This is useful for creating historical scenarios where players have predetermined alliances. For example, in a World War 2 scenario, Germany, Italy, and Japan would be grouped into the "Axis" team, while the US, Britian, and the USSR would be put into the "Allies" team.
