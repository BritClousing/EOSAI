Quick Tour of Empires of Steel

Play

Empires of Steel is a turn-based wargame. Players take the role of a dictator/general in control of a nation and commanding military forces against other nations. The goal of the game is to take control of the game world, either as a single player or as a team.

New users can learn how to play using the tutorial.

Games can be played on randomly generated maps, user-created maps, or user-created scenarios.

Games can be played against computer players, or human players (over the internet or a LAN).

Built-in voicechat lets players talk to allies and enemies.


Map/Scenario Editor

The built-in map and scenario editor lets players create their own maps and scenarios. Maps define the geography and placement of cities and resources. An example of a map is a map of the earth - there are no predefined nations; just continents, cities, resources, and terrain. Scenarios are like maps, except they let the user setup nations - who owns which cities, what units they control, and what technologies they know. An example of a scenario would be a World War 2 scenario - a world map with five predefined players: German, Japanese, American, British, and Soviet, along with the cities, units, and technologies appropriate to each player.


Ruleset Editor

The built-in ruleset editor lets players create their own technology trees and units. For example, a ruleset might define technologies appropriate to a World War 2 simulation by defining technologies and units available during the 1939-1945 period. It's even possible to create nation-specific units and technologies - for example, a Kamikazi unit available exclusively to the Japanese.


Upload/Download User-Created Content

Users can upload and download maps, scenarios, and rulesets for/from other players. Users can also score and comment on user-created content, which helps identify the best user-content for new users.
